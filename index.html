Experience the rich history and vibrant culture of Mauritius in the heart of Port Louis. We invite you to explore our first open-air museum which has beautifully preserved historical sites, and serene natural landscapes. Immerse yourself in the stories of our island, where every corner holds a piece of our shared heritage.

Our History Discoveries

The postal service in Mauritius started in 1772 under French rule, using slaves to deliver letters. There were no dedicated postal buildings, just a delivery service. Visit our history page to know more about Mauritius treasures.

Place D’Armes is renowned as the heart of Port Louis, majestically lined with tall palm trees and surrounded by old cannons partially buried in the ground.

Frequently Asked Questions Contact Us

What are the opening hours and admission fees?

Our open-air museum is open to visitors from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM on weekdays and from 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM on weekends.

Is there an admission fee to enter the open-air museum, or is it free?

Yes, visit our FAQ page for more info or book your ticket online on our website.

Is photography allowed inside the museum?

Photography allowed in specified areas; Please follow posted guidelines. Check signage or ask staff for area-specific rules.

Are there any facilities such as restrooms, cafes, or gift shops?

Yes, since our open-air museum is located in Port Louis, a vibrant public area, you will find cafes and gift shops all around.

How can we book a guided tour in advance?

You can book a guided tour by visiting our website or contacting our visitor services.
