Plan your School trip

A school trip to our first Open-Air Museum is about discovering, learning, and having fun together. This museum offers various experiences where both primary and secondary school students can dive into the history and culture of Mauritius. We don't just tell you about the past; we let your students experience the differences and similarities between then and now. Additionally, we explore the future and its possibilities, making the learning journey even more enriching.

Primary Trip Education

For primary schools, the Port Louis Open-Air Museum offers a variety of engaging and educational activities designed to spark curiosity and foster a love for history and culture. Our programs include interactive guided tours that are specially tailored to introduce young learners to the rich history and vibrant culture of Mauritius. Through storytelling during these tours, students gain a deeper understanding of the past. They also experience historical reenactments, witnessing significant events and daily life in old Port Louis firsthand, which provides them with insights into how people lived, worked, and played in the past. Additionally, storytelling sessions delve into Mauritius's history, exploring key events, cultural milestones, and significant figures that have shaped the island's identity. These narratives aim to cultivate a deeper appreciation for Mauritius's cultural heritage and provide insights into its historical evolution. Age-appropriate educational exhibits further enhance the learning experience, covering various aspects of Mauritian history, including the arrival of different cultures and the evolution of the island's society.

Secondary Education

For secondary education, the Port Louis Open-Air Museum offers an enriching educational experience focused on delving deeper into Mauritius's diverse history and cultural heritage. Our programs are designed to engage students in critical thinking and historical analysis through: Exploration of the historical sites within the museum, where students can learn about significant events such as the abolition of slavery and the arrival of indentured laborers, gaining a deeper understanding of Mauritius's transition from colonialism to independence. Interactive workshops that allow students to engage with artifacts and primary sources, encouraging them to interpret historical evidence and draw connections between past and present societies. Guided tours that explore the complex layers of Mauritian identity, including its multicultural influences and socio-economic developments over time. Through these educational opportunities, students not only deepen their historical knowledge but also develop critical thinking skills and a nuanced understanding of Mauritius's cultural heritage.
